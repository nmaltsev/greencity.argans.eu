Our services offer a unique end-to-end management solution to support municipal planting strategies according to "best practices" recommendations.

ARGANS acquire commercial Very High Resolution (VHR) satellite images, which have a spatial resolution of less than 50cm, that enables us to detect and identify individual urban trees and to classify them accordingly e.g., species, height, crown diameter etc.

Satellite remote sensing allows us to do this for the entire city, and prepare an accurate chart of green spaces within the city and a detailed inventory of the public and private individual trees, shrubs and grass (lawn/turf).

Knowledge of species and characteristics of the urban greenery allows us to estimate the environmental benefits of the urban tree heritage in measurable units at the scale of the entire city.

We provide quantified estimates of carbon storage, carbon dioxide sequestration, and air pollutant removal capacity in units of tons of nitrogen dioxide, ozone, and particles per year.

To quantify the benefits we provide charts of Urban Heat Islands for the entire city and an estimate of thermal comfort (Universal Thermal Comfort Index).

ARGANS are not only providing the eye from the sky perspective. Our skilled staff offer consultancy services providing 'best practice' recommendations for urban regreening projects.

Depending on your territory and environmental conditions ARGANS provides a list of suitable species to plant for healthier air in the city and open areas available. Each tree species is assessed for its resistance to local conditions (e.g., drought tolerance, pests and diseases), biological characteristics and capacity to absorb air pollutants.

We also provide training to authorities in the development of Urban Greening Plans and monitoring the execution and efficacy.

To fully appreciate the benefits of an Urban Greening Plan, ARGANS quantifies the socio-economic benefits derived from planting programs in a given area in units policy makers understand: reduction in premature deaths and hospitalizations for respiratory and cardiovascular illnesses and the related costs.

About Us

ARGANS Limited, is a fast-growing UK SME that has a reputation for excellence in delivering Earth Observation satellite remote sensing applications and services, innovative solutions in data analytics, Geographical Information Systems and the use of remote sensing data to support satellite derived bathymetry, coastal charting and mapping, climate change monitoring, environmental information, and geospatial intelligence.

Dr Pierre Sicard

Expert on the impact assessment of air pollution and climate change on vegetation and urban forestry.

Co-ordinator & Lead Scientist

Dr Pierre Sicard, who has 80 peer-reviewed publications, is Deputy Coordinator of the RG 8.04.00 "Air Pollution & Climate Change" under the International Union of Forest Research Organizations (IUFRO) and involved as UNECE Expert Panel on Clean Air in Cities and active in the EU Clean Air Forum

ARGANS, a member of the ACRI Group, is based in Plymouth, UK with offices in Sophia-Antipolis and Toulouse, France.
